Chemical Peeling

Medical chemical peels that resurface dull skin, refine texture and even out tone.

About the treatment

A medical chemical peel uses precisely formulated acids to dissolve the bonds holding dull, damaged surface cells — prompting the skin to renew itself fresher, smoother and more even. Depth is everything: from no-downtime glow peels to deeper resurfacing for pigmentation and acne marks.

Your doctor selects the peel family (fruit acids, salicylic, TCA blends and more), the strength and the number of layers based on your skin and your calendar — because great peel results are planned, not improvised.

Benefits

  • Brightens dull skin and evens out tone
  • Softens post-acne marks and pigmentation
  • Refines texture, pores and fine lines
  • Boosts the effect of your home skincare
  • Scalable depth — from lunchtime glow to true resurfacing

Recovery & downtime

Depends entirely on depth. Superficial peels: a day of mild pinkness, perhaps light flaking. Medium peels: 3–7 days of visible peeling — plan around social events. Diligent SPF 50+ is non-negotiable after any peel, and we schedule deeper peels away from strong sun seasons.

Who is it for?

Dullness, uneven tone, sun damage, post-acne marks, rough texture — peels address them all. Not performed on active infections or over-sensitised skin; your consultation confirms the right formula and timing.

Frequently asked questions

Will my face visibly peel?
Only with medium-depth peels — and we will tell you exactly what to expect before we start. Superficial glow peels usually cause little to no visible flaking.
Do peels hurt?
You will feel warmth and tingling for a few minutes, closely monitored and fully tolerable. Deeper peels are buffered and timed precisely by your doctor.
Can peels be done in summer?
Superficial peels yes, with strict SPF. Deeper peels are better planned for lower-sun months — we will build the right calendar with you.
